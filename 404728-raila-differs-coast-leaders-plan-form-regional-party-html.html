Raila differs with coast leaders over plan to form regional party

Publish date: 2024-05-03

- Raila Odinga warned that the formation of a regional party might alienate the region from the rest of the country

- The seasoned politician, however, said it was in the best interest for regional leaders to come together to tackle certain issues as a bloc

- A new political outfit for the coast region could be unveiled in June 2021 if the recent developments are anything to go by

The Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga has urged coast politicians to go slow in their quest to form a regional party.

Raila, who kicked off a five-day tour of the region, noted although Kenya was a democratic country, regional political outfits may not serve the intended purpose of unity.

"Kenya is a multi-party democracy and we want to have national political parties that will unite this country. Regional political outfits will only serve to divide," he warned.

Speaking in Voi on Tuesday, March 2, the seasoned politician said it was in the best interest of regional leaders to tackle certain issues as a bloc.

"The leaders have a right to speak about regional politics and issues that are dear to them because Kenya is a democratic country. If you have seen there is economic bloc that brings together counties around Lake Victoria. There is another for Mt Kenya. However, I advise them to be careful not to segregate the region from the rest of the country," he added.

New outfit

A new political outfit for the coast region could be unveiled in June 2021 if the recent revelations are anything to go by.

Governors Amason Kingi (Kilifi) and Mombasa colleague Hassan Ali Joho could ditch ODM for the regional outfit ahead of the 2022 General Election.

Speaking separately earlier in the year, the two politicians said the time had come for the coast residents to board one political vehicle to champion for perennial issues bedevilling the region.

“Coast region should stop being over-reliant on parties from other regions. I want to assure the coast people that, come June, we shall have our own outfit that will be used to spearhead the region’s political agenda,” Kingi said on Saturday, January 9, during burial of former MP Gunga Mwinga in Kaloleni.

A similar call for regional unity through the formation of a single political party had also dominated the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) sensitisation forum in Garsen on Thursday 7.

“It is high time coast people charted their own political destiny, we shall no longer be the slaves of people who have been using us for their own political gain. If you look at Hassan Joho, do you think I am not fit to contest for president?" Joho posed.
