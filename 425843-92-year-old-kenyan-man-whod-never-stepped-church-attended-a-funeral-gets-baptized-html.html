92-Year-Old Fisherman Who'd Never Stepped in Church, Attended a Funeral Gets Baptised

Publish date: 2024-02-07
  • After 92 years of avoiding Christianity, Owuor Nyapoya recently went to church and got baptised
  • Father Stephen Ouma, the man of God who welcomed him into religion, baptised him as Paul to show a new leaf had been turned over
  • Nyapoya disclosed that he finally decided to change his ways as he's hit an age that only shows he is blessed

In the 92 years that Owuor Nyapoya from Usenge in Siaya county has lived, he's never set foot in church nor attended a funeral.

It is a period that the nonagenarian fisherman remained a man of the world.

That, however, came to a stop recently when he made the decision to walk to St Martin Catholic Church and get baptised.

According to the Sunday Standard, Nyapoya was donned in a white shirt, grey trousers, and a pair of black shoes.

According to Father Stephen Ouma, who is in charge of the Parish, Nyapoya was the oldest person he had ever baptised in all his years of being a man of the cloth.

Spent many years in Uganda

Nyapoya revealed that he decided to take charge of his spiritual life because he's lived long enough to feel luckier than many individuals.

“Not many people of my age survived the HIV and Aids pandemic because of the kind of lifestyle we lived. There was a lot of food, money and women,” he noted.

He remembered the days gone by when he turned down all attempts by family and friends to convince him to get saved.

Nyapoya disclosed that he has never visited a hospital and didn't attend formal school because his father did not attach value to education.

“We were herding our father’s animals and when I became an adult, I ventured into fishing because that was the only economic activity that majority were doing other than farming,” he said.

The baptism opens a new journey for Nyapoya, one that will hopefully take away the hate and evil that he says had filled his heart.

Baptised as Paul

Like in the Bible, Father Stephen Ouma baptised Nyapoya as Paul, opening a new chapter in his Christian life.

The man of God noted that he gave the new convert a new name as it symbolizes Biblical change.

“Paul was up and down but finally saw the light and changed. This old man gave consent and I had no option but to prepare him to the kingdom of God,” Father Ouma said.

Now that he has a baptismal medal hanging from his neck, Nyapoya looks at the future with pride as he takes the road into the Christian faith.

Father Ouma explained that it was inspiring for the new convert to seek salvation especially at such an advanced age of his life.

He also added that old men like Nyapoya do not necessarily need to attend catechism classes before being baptised.

Seth Mahiga resigns from Atheism

Nyapoya's case is reminiscent of Seth Mahiga's, the one-time chairperson of the Atheists in Kenya Society (AIK) who resigned from the position.

He noted that he had found Jesus Christ and was, therefore, no longer interested in promoting atheism.

Mahiga's resignation elicited mixed reactions on social media with many congratulating him for the bold decision.
