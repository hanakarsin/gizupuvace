Uhuru Must Declare Money Used in BBI, Refund Kenyans, Lawyer Morara Omoke Demands

Publish date: 2024-06-05

- City lawyer Morara Omoke has demanded that all the funds used by the state in the BBI process be refunded

- Morara Omoke's application had previously been filed in the High Court but later dismissed before he filed it at the Court of Appeal

- On Thursday, May 13, five high court judges declared the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) illegitimate

The row surrounding the implementation of the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) has taken a new turn after lawyer Morara Omoke petitioned the Court of Appeal to compel President Uhuru Kenyatta to publicly state the amount of money that was used in all activities surrounding the process.

Morara wants Uhuru to personally refund the National Treasury public funds used by the BBI steering committee to promote the Constitution Of Kenya Amendment Bill, 2020.

Morara's application had previously been filed in the High Court but later dismissed.

Hearing of the BBI appeal case

Court of Appeal President David Musinga announced that a seven-judge bench would hear all appeal cases surrounding the BBI process.

The appeal will be heard for four days, from June 29 to July 2, 2021.

Musinga further warned parties against prosecuting the case in media.

The Appellate Court President said that there was a need to conduct the proceedings with the dignity that is required.

"Let us be civil. Let us respect one another. Let us be balanced in our comments. Let us also realise that we have an important judicial process that must be handled in a very sober manner," he explained.

He called all parties involved to allow the court to make a fair, properly informed decision without prosecuting the media.

"Let us not prosecute or defend our positions outside there in the media. There should be no unnecessary attack by all concerned parties," he added.

Null and void

On Thursday, May 13, five high court judges declared the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) illegitimate.

The High Court ruled Uhuru initiated the BBI in contravention to Article 257 of the constitution.

Uhuru, his handshake partner Raila and Attorney-General Kihara Kariuki filed formal appeals against the High Court verdict.

Through Solicitor General Kennedy Ogeto, the AG listed 31 issues he noted the judges erred in law and fact by declaring the BBI unconstitutional.

The president, on the other hand, through his lead lawyer Waweru Gatonye filed a 10-page formal appeal raising different points why the judgment should be overturned.
