Remdesivir: Health Ministry to use newly registered COVID-19 drug in small doses

Publish date: 0001-01-01

- Health director general Patrick Amoth said there was still no absolute cure for coronavirus pandemic despite the US approving the registration of remdesivir drug

- The health official said it had been confirmed patients who used the drug recovered in 11 days compared to 15 days for those who used placebo

- As of Saturday, May 2, evening, 435 cases of COVID-19 had been confirmed in Kenya with 22 deaths recorded

The Health Ministry has said it will use Remdesivir - the newly registered COVID-19 drug in small doses in the treatment of coronavirus patients in Kenya.

Health Ministry's Director General Patrick Amoth said there was still no absolute cure for the pandemic despite the US registering the drug for treatment of COVID-19.

Speaking during the daily coronavirus briefing on Saturday, May 2, Amoth said it had been confirmed patients who used the drug recovered in 11 days compared to 15 days for those who used placebo.

"Remdevisir is not a silver bullet. It can only be given as infusion and is relatively expensive. Most of our cases are mild and asymptomatic- so this drug can only be admitted in small doses. We would continue using other drugs including chloroquine- which are available locally," Amoth said.

A study by the US government revealed Remdesivir interfered with the replication of some viruses, including the SARS-CoV-2 virus responsible for the current pandemic.

“The data shows that Remdesivir has a clear-cut significant positive effect in diminishing the time to recovery,” Dr Anthony Fauci, the US' top infectious-disease expert said during a White House press conference on Wednesday, April 29.

The drug was rapidly pushed through clinical trials due to ebola virus epidemic of 2013–2016, eventually being used in people with the disease.

It was used in the emergency setting during the Kivu, DRC ebola epidemic.

As of Saturday, May 2, evening, 435 cases of COVID-19 had been confirmed in Kenya with 22 deaths recorded.

This came after Health CAS Mercy Mwangangi confirmed 24 new cases.
