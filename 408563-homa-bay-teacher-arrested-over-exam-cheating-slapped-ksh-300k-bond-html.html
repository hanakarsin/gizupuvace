Homa Bay: Teacher Arrested Over Exam Cheating Slapped with KSh 300k Bond

Publish date: 2024-03-17

- Calvin Magolo, a teacher at Nyagwethe Secondary, was arraigned for perpetrating exam cheating

- Magolo appeared before Homa Bay Principal Magistrate Joy Wesonga where he denied the charges

- He was slapped with KSh 300k bond pending hearing of the case on May 5, 2021

A secondary school teacher arrested in Homa Bay county for abetting exam cheating has been released on a KSh 300,000 bail.

The suspect Calvin Magolo, a teacher at Nyagwethe Secondary School in Suba Sub County, was arraigned in Homa Bay for engaging in exam malpractices contrary to the Kenya National Examination Council (KNEC) laws.

Magolo appeared before Homa Bay Principal Magistrate Joy Wesonga on Tuesday, April 13, where he denied the charges.

The teacher was accused that on April 7, within Mbita Town, he was found in possession of two mobile phones which contained contents of CRE Paper 1 and Kiswahili Paper 3.

The court heard that the accused had already sent the exam content to seven different phone numbers.

He pleaded not guilty and was released on a bond of KSh 300,0000 with a surety of a similar amount.

The case will be heard on May 5, 2021.

This came few days after Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha accused some exam centre managers and teachers of encouraging exam malpractices.

Magoha noted that some centre manager and supervisors had allowed the vice to happen under their nose in the ongoing Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education tests.

“Exam centre managers must stop being gullible and give children fake materials that only confuse them more. The government will not let you go without subjecting you to appropriate punishment,” Magoha warned.

He disclosed the government had learnt some headteachers in charge of the exam centres were striving to leak papers.
