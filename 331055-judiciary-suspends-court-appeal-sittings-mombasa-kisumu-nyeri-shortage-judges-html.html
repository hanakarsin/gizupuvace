Judiciary suspends Court of Appeal sittings in Mombasa, Kisumu, Nyeri over shortage of judges

Publish date: 2024-04-13

- There are only 15 out of 27 judges at the Court of Appeal spread out in the country

- The court's president Ouko attributed the situation to death and retirement cases

- The judicial commission recommended 11 judges to fill in the positions

- President Uhuru Kenyatta is yet to gazette the names for the coveted positions

The Judiciary has announced suspension of Court of Appeal sittings outside Nairobi owing to low number of judges.

Consequently, judges who are stationed in Mombasa, Nyeri and Kisumu will be relocated to Nairobi effective January 13, 2020, in view of the evolving circumstance.

Currently, there are only 15 appellate judges spread out across the country from the initial 27.

These include the president of the appellate court, the court's representative to the Judicial Service Commission and the director of the Judiciary Training Institute.

The president William Ouko explained in a statement on Friday, December 20, that only 12 judges sit full time with Nairobi court alone handling nearly 3000 appeals and applications daily.

"In consultation with the Chief Justice, it has been decided that as long as the situation with regard to the number of judges in the Court of Appeal remains, the sittings outside Nairobi are suspended with effect from January 13, 2020," Ouko stated.

However, the situation has also been exacerbated by the demise of Kisumu appellate judge Otieno Odek and retirements.

The commission recommended at least 11 individuals for the coveted positions in July 2019, but President Uhuru Kenyatta is yet to gazette their names.

In August 2018, Chief Justice David Maraga placed a request before the judicial commission to fill in the vacancies thereby raising the number of judges from the current 27.
