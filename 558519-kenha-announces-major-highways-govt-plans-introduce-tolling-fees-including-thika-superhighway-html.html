KeNHA Announces Major Highways Govt Plans to Introduce Tolling Fees Including Thika Superhighway

Publish date: 2024-05-17
  • The Kenya National Highways Authority (KeNHA) noted it would engage the public on the Road Tolling Policy
  • It explained that once the policy is finalised, it will guide on the roads that will be tolled, including the Nairobi-Nakuru-Mau Summit Highway
  • Tolling is a form of road pricing typically implemented to help recoup the costs of road construction and maintenance

TUKO.co.ke journalist Japhet Ruto has over eight years of experience in financial, business, and technology reporting and offers profound insights into Kenyan and global economic trends.

The Kenya National Highways Authority (KeNHA) plans to introduce tolling fees on major highways in Nairobi, Mombasa, and Nakuru.

Which roads will be tolled in Kenya?

In a statement on its official X handle, KeNHA noted it would engage the public on the Road Tolling Policy.

The government agency said that once the policy is finalised, it will guide on the roads that will be tolled.

"The Kenya National Highways Authority appreciates the ongoing discussion around the development of a Road Tolling Policy. The authority assures the public that this policy will be developed with extensive public participation to ensure that all voices are heard. Once the Policy is finalised, it will guide which roads will be tolled," it stated.

According to KeNHA, the following highways are being considered for tolling:

  • Nairobi Southern Bypass.
  • Nairobi-Nakuru-Mau Summit Highway.
  • Thika Superhighway.
  • Kenol-Sagana-Marua Road.
  • Mombasa Southern Bypass.
  • Dongo Kundu Bypass.

What road tolling means in Kenya

A toll road is a public or private road for which a fee (or toll) is assessed for passage.

The new proposed law seeks to implement the Public Roads Toll Act, Cap 407, which gives the Treasury the power to declare any road or a portion a toll road.

What are the charges on the Nairobi Expressway?

Earlier, TUKO.co.ke reported that the government announced a 50% increase in the Nairobi Expressway toll charges.

The Ministry of Transport approved the new charges in a gazette notice released on December 19, 2023.

Motorists now pay KSh 500 from Westlands Nairobi to Mlolongo for a one-way trip.

The ministry said the charges were subject to changes based on the exchange rate and the Consumer Price Index (CPI).
