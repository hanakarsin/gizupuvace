KPC boss admits corruption deals still happening at corporation

Publish date: 2024-02-03

- KPC acting boss Andambi said the organisation was encountering many irregularities that have led to huge loss of money

- An audit report indicated the corporation engaged in questionable dealings with KenolKobil and purchased motor vehicle parts at inflated prices

- Andambi indicated that his team was ready to cooperate with investigative agencies to ensure the graft racket is uncovered

Serious revelations of corruption at troubled Kenya Pipeline Company (KPC) have continued to be revealed after acting CEO Hudson Andambi appeared before Parliament’s Public Investment Committee.

The acting boss acknowledged some top officers at the parastatal were still engaging in questionable deals even after their counterparts were charged in court.

Appearing before the committee interrogating the 2017/2018 audit report on the parastatal on Thursday, October 3, Andambi said the organisation was encountering many irregularities that have led to huge loss of money.

"We are more than willing to collaborate with investigating agencies because there is nothing to hide. We are determined to achieve the presidential directive of delivering mwananchi gas cylinders to all citizens by 2020. We cannot achieve that with the impropriety at the corporation,” he said.

According to the auditor general’s report, KPC management engaged in questionable dealings with KenolKobil Company including through irregular purchases of motor vehicle parts at inflated prices.

The CEO was forced to testify under oath with the committee chair AbdulSwamad Shariff Nasir after it also emerged that some board members had a conflict of interest but were still allowed to make decisions in the company.

Andambi was appointed interim Managing Director of KPC after Joe Sang and other officials were arraigned and faced corruption related charges.

They were linked to questionable construction of a KSh 1.8 billion Kisumu Oil Jetty and dubious spillage claims which saw the company lose over 20 million litres valued at about KSh 2.3 billion.

Elsewhere, National Assembly Committee on Energy has demanded Principal Secretary for Petroleum and Mining Andrew Kamau to produce documents of communication between KPC and Public Service Commission that led to re-advertisement of the corporation’s CEO position.

Appearing before the energy committee to respond to queries of impropriety at KPC, acting committee chairperson Robert Pukose said claims by Kamau that only three out of 140 individuals who applied for the job had Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC) clearance were questionable.

Pukose said Kamau’s pronouncement was illogical to any citizen with a sound mind.

“We want KPC board of directors to come here and explain to members, on which grounds did they reach the re-advertisement decision. They must submit before this committee, names and details of the recruiting personnel,” Pukose said.

Story by Aron Ndonyi, TUKO.co.ke Correspondent, Nairobi
