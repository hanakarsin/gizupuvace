Seasoned trade unionist Francis Atwoli has retained his seat as Central Organisation of Trade Unions (COTU) secretary-general after he was re-elected unopposed for the 5th term.

Atwoli will now serve for a term of five years after COTU delegates unanimously endorsed him after his challenger Kenya National Union of Nurses’ secretary-general Seth Panyako pulled out.

He has been at the helm of COTU since 2001 when he succeeded the late Joseph Mugalla.

Taking to his official Twitter page on Friday, April 9, Atwoli thanked all Kenyan workers for rallying behind his candidacy.

“I want to thank all Kenyan worker for re-electing me unopposed, through their registered Trade Union Representatives, as the Secretary-General of @COTU_K. I promise to service you with all my might and strength and not betray the trust you have bestowed in me," Atwoli tweeted.

In his role as a representative of Kenyan workers, Atwoli has expressed criticism of politicians on issues such as their refusal to pay taxes on their allowances and corruption.

The 71-year-old Atwoli has also been critical of non-governmental bodies and global institutions including the World Bank, interfering with labour disputes.

He openly supports police officers when they advocate for the creation of a police officers’ union.

While on a tour of his Nakuru home in January Atwoli had revealed his retirement plans.

In February 2021, the COTU boss condemned a move by the government to increase fuel prices amid high taxation and the poor state of the economy amid the COVID-19 pandemic.

"COTU do strongly condemn the move by the Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority for its decision to adjust prices for petroleum products by all-time high at a time many Kenyans are facing the most difficult moments in their life owing to the hard economic times the country faces today. This will have a domino effect on the cost of living in Kenya with transportation and prices of goods going up," said Atwoli.

Before his election to COTU, Atwoli served as general secretary of the Kenya Plantation and Agricultural Workers Union.

He has also served as director of the Central Organisation of Trade Unions, Kenya.
