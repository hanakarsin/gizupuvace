Churches Have Become Epicenters of Immorality, Corruption and Death

Publish date: 2024-06-30

In January 2020, the country was hit by the nerve-wracking news of a Mombasa-based pastor who walked to the pulpit in the middle of prayers and murdered his wife before God and man.

The preacher, armed with long knives, cut his own throat shortly after and died with the wife.

The two were reportedly fighting over the control of the church, whose membership had started to shrink.

The preacher felt the wife was determined to elbow him out and take charge of the church.

That is how low the church in Kenya has sunk. It has lost its hallowed place as a beacon of hope and virtue, increasingly degenerating and becoming synonymous with wickedness.

Preachers, who were once revered by society, have become not only false prophets but also merchants of evil.

The outrageous and scandalous things endlessly unfolding in the church today are simply disturbing. The devil appears to be on the loose, hurling morality and sanity out of church windows.

Churches have become battlegrounds where blood is unashamedly shed. Shepherds have been engaging in physical fights, hiring goons to cause mayhem and even eliminating perceived competitors.

Immorality

Other than breeding chaos and disorder, the church has also been heavily rocked by cases of immorality.

Men of the cloth have been accused of snatching other people’s wives and in some cases, caught red-handed preying on their follower’s wives or daughters. Others have been implicated in defilement cases. In January this year, a Kirinyaga pastor was jailed for 140 years for defiling his two daughters.

Last year, a Migori-based preacher was arrested for defiling and impregnating a girl aged 14 years at Ongito village in Nyatike. A month later, another ‘man of God’ from Homa Bay was arrested for defiling a Class Eight pupil.

About two years ago, a man from Kiambu accused a pastor who presided over his wedding of eloping with his wife of 26 years.

John Kanyua, a father of five, claimed the pastor’s relationship with his wife started when he appointed her an assistant pastor of a church in Juja Farm.

Preachers have also sexualised their preaching and centres of worship to attract worshippers. A renowned city preacher has notoriously been promoting this vice for close to a decade and has been hit with several scandals. Others have been organising sexual brothels in places of worship, all in God’s name.

Fake Miracles

The names of dozens of fake prophets and preachers conning unsuspecting believers through fake miracles and testimonies have been written on the wall for whoever cares to see to do so.

They purport to heal the lame, restore the sight of the visually impaired, cast away evil spirits and even resurrect the dead, all in the form of stage-managed antics aimed at conning believers.

In 2017, a self-proclaimed prophet purportedly resurrected a woman from West Pokot and duped his followers into believing the hoax. A few years later, the same woman died and was buried as the con preacher watched.

The church has brainwashed millions of believers into believing in miracles to excel. Many hold the view that ‘giving’ supersedes hard work; some end up selling their property to ‘bless’ their pastors so they can receive miracles.

Corruption and conmanship

Preachers are openly taking advantage of their followers’ loyalty to swindle them of their hard-earned finances to the very last coin.

In March 2019, a city preacher known for his miracle antics was accused of using unorthodox antics to brainwash and dispossess one of his followers of property worth billions of shillings.

One of the high-end properties the pastor was accused of robbing his follower is the Dove Apartments located in the upmarket Westlands. It is approximated to be worth KSh 1.5 billion.

The property has become a subject of a police probe after the family of the said victim raised the matter.

A host of other city preachers are living lavishly courtesy of conning and defrauding their followers.

They unashamedly demand contributions from congregants and scare them with tragedies if they don’t give a certain amount of money to ‘God’.

Their followers and the media have exposed many. Victor Kanyari of the Salvation Healing Ministry has been at the centre of such sagas.

Politics and church donations

The church has also terribly failed the moral test of rejecting ill-gotten money, especially from known corrupt politicians who hop from one church to another with sacks of money.

Instead of condemning corruption, the church has become an avenue of promoting the vice through harambees organised for politicians to sell their agenda and buy votes.

Men of the cloth have also failed to resist the temptation of remaining apolitical and have readily taken sides even as political tension continues to rise ahead of 2022.

The church is the House of God and is meant to be a place of prayer, hope, sanity and spiritual nourishment. Preachers are supposed to be role models devoted to sending the faithful to heaven, and not hell.

Believers

Believers must start assessing and questioning the conduct of their preachers.

John 4:1 says :

“Dear friends, do not believe every spirit, but test the spirits to see whether they are from God, because many false prophets have gone out into the world”.

The Bible is also clear of the fate that awaits to lead others to sin.

Luke 17, 2 Jesus stated thus:

“It is inevitable that stumbling blocks will come, but woe to the one through whom they come! It would be better for him to have a millstone hung around his neck and to be thrown into the sea than to cause one of these little ones to stumble”.
