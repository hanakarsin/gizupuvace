Kenyans stand with MP Moses Kuria after being roughed up during Kitui BBI forum

Publish date: 2024-03-14

- Moses Kuria and Kipchumba arrived at the rally on a boda bodas

- The MPs did not find seats and remained standing as organisers tried to find space for them

- Soon anti-Kuria chants rented the air and was ejected out of the rally

Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria had an embarrassing moment when a section of youth ejected him from the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) rally in Kitui on Saturday, February 1.

However, Kenyan online community have taken issues with the incident which was captured on camera terming it despicable and against the spirit of the BBI recommendations.

"Surely what was done to Moses Kuria today in Kitui is unacceptable..it is Breaking Bridges Initiative instead of Building Bridges Initiative.The youths asked for a seat to be given to him..Kudos youths of Kitui- Eastern region. Elected leaders should be respected every where," observed Eric Malel.

The drama started soon after Kuria accompanied by Elgeyo Marakwet senator Kipchumba Murkomen arrived at the BBI rally in boda boda.

This, was when every politician including chief guest ODM leader Raila Odinga had settled and it was time for Governor Kivutha Kibwana to read the Eastern region declaration.

However, the Gatundu lawmaker and Kipchumba remained standing behind the main dais for awhile as the organisers tried for find spaces for them.

It did not take long as anti-Kuria soon rented the air forcing area Governor Charity Ngilu to grab microphone from Kivutha, she warned no politician insulting President Uhuru Kenyatta will be allowed in.

While a section of Kenyans sympathised with the second time legislator, others claimed he had attended the function with a view to disrupting considering he was against the document.
