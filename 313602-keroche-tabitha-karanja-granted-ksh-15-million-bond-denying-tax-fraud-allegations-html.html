Keroche Breweries: Tabitha Karanja granted KSh 15 million bond in tax fraud case

Publish date: 2024-06-08

- Tabitha Karanja who is the CEO of Keroche Breweries was slapped with a KSh 15 billion bond or a cash bail of KSh 10 million

- Joseph Karanja, the second respondent, was granted KSh 5 million or an alternative cash bail of KSh 2 million

- The court directed Joseph as well as Keroche Breweries to make the payments within seven days or risk arrest

- Chief Magistrate Francis Andayi granted the second respondent lenient terms owing to his health complications

Keroche Breweries director Tabitha Karanja has been granted a KSh 15 million bond after denying tax evasion charges.

Tabitha's husband, Joseph Karanja, who was the second respondent was released on a bond of KSh 5 million or an alternative cash bail of KSh 2 million.

In a ruling delivered on Friday, August 23, the court further granted Keroche industries, the third respondent, a bail term of KSh 15 million.

Chief Magistrate Francis Andayi granted Joseph a lenient bond after a doctor's report indicated his health condition was wanting.

“I'm avoiding him going to custody and meeting his maker there and say it is the court which did this,” Andayi said

The court directed the second and third respondents to pay the respective bonds within seven days, failure to which all the directors, except Joseph, would be arrested.

Tabitha was, however, expected to comply with the terms before the end of the day.

The prosecution had asked the court to deny the suspects bail terms and instead detain them for seven days pending further investigations.

Defence counsel led by James Orengo, however, objected the prosecution's appeal stating it amounted to violation of their rights.

Orengo told the court his clients would comply with the bail terms and there was no need to confiscate their travel documents arguing the accused were investors in the country.

In his ruling, Andayi noted there was no convincing reasons to deny the suspects bail but instead ordered them to submit their travel documents in court.

The suspects were accused of evading tax to a tune of KSh 14 billion
