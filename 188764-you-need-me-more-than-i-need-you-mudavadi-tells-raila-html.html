Raila and Mudavadi clash over CORD's presidential candidate

Publish date: 2024-02-29

- Opposition leader Raila Odinga and ANC leader Musalia Mudavadi are in a war of words on the 2017 elections

- Mudavadi has told Raila that he is not begging to be in CORD

- Raila, on the other hand, termed those expecting endorsements from him as crazy

CORD leader Raila Odinga and Amani party leader Musalia Mudavadi are caught up in a war of words on the next general election.

READ ALSO: Raila Odinga shocks politicians seeking his support

The back and forth between the two leaders is about CORD's presidential candidate in 2017 elections.

Mudavadi has responded to Raila Odinga following his comments on those seeking endorsements from him for 2017.

READ ALSO: Raila speaks on disbanding CORD, parting ways with Kalonzo and Wetangula

READ ALSO: Why Raila loses support among his lieutenants – Mudavadi

The statement angered Mudavadi who told Raila Odinga that he is not begging to be in the CORD coalition.

He further alleged that the Raila will run on an ODM ticket and not CORD saying that is why he is promoting three-piece voting for ODM.

Raila’s remarks came after speculations emerged that he could endorse Ford Kenya's Moses Wetangula, Kalonzo Musyoka or Musalia Mudavadi to be the coalition’s flag bearer in the presidential elections.
