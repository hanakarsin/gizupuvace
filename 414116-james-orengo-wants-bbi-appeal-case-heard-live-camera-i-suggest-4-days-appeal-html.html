Siaya senator James Orengo who doubles up as a lawyer has pleaded with the Court of Appeal to have the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) case heard live on camera.

While making his submissions at the Appellate Court on Wednesday, June 2, the senior counsel asked for a faster hearing of the appeal as time was running out.

"Looking at the judgment, there were nearly 23 declarations made affecting our two clients, the hearing of this petition will be done in the full glare of the public, given that it is about our constitution, our suggestion would be four days for appeal," Orengo argued.

The veteran lawyer who has been described by some as the 'walking constitution' is representing former prime minister Raila Odinga.

He will argue the case alongside Otiende Amollo, Paul Mwangi, Jackson Awele, Ben Sihanya and Arnold Oginga who are other lawyers in the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader's team.

The lawyers appeared before a three-judge appellate bench purposely to deliberate on the timeline and logistics relating to the hearing of the BBI appeal case.

Seven-judge bench to hear appeals

Court of Appeal president Justice Daniel Musinga said the appeal case would be heard by a seven-judge bench from the end of June or early July.

On Thursday, May 13, five high court judges declared the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) illegitimate.

The High Court ruled the BBI was initiated by President Uhuru Kenyatta in contravention to Article 257 of the constitution.

Uhuru, his handshake partner Raila and Attorney-General Kihara Kariuki have since filed formal appeals against the High Court verdict.

The AG through Solicitor General Kennedy Ogeto listed 31 issues he noted the judges erred in law and fact by declaring the BBI unconstitutional.

The president on the other hand through his lead lawyer Waweru Gatonye filed a 10-page formal appeal raising different points why the judgment should be overturned.
