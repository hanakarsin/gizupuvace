Police officers to sit for exams as outgoing IG Joseph Boinnet rules out automatic promotions

Publish date: 2024-04-29

- All police officers will be subjected national exams supervised by Kenya National Police Service Examination Board

- The initiative which was officially launched by Interior CS Fred Matiang'i aims at enhancing transparency within the service

- The officers will be subjected to the same curriculum across the country

- Joseph Boinet said the program will start in May 2019

- Directorate of Criminal Investigation (DCI) George Kinoti lauded the program saying it will help restore police dignity

Government has launched a program that will see all police officers sit for national exams supervised by the Kenya National Police Service Examination Board (KNPSEB) in a bid to instill discipline and enhance integrity within the sector.

The program was launched by Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i who said promotion of police officers will be based on performance in the exams, ruling out automatic annual elevations.

Speaking during the launch of the examination boar at Kenya School of Government on Thursday, March 28, Boinnet said the new program would help minimise corruption cases witnessed in the service during promotion and transfer of officers.

"This process will not only impart police officer with necessary skills in how to deal with the dynamic issues the encounter, but will also help reduce cases of corruption witnessed during promotion and transfer of officers," he said

The police service will partner with Kenya Institute of Curriculum Development to effectively roll out the program across the country.

Boinnet said implementation of the curriculum will start in May 2019.

Directorate of Criminal Investigation (DCI) boss George Kinoti termed the initiative timely and necessary in restoring the dignity of police service in the country.

"My commitment has been to restore dignity the the police service and such an initiative is highly welcome since it augurs well with my transformative agenda in the service," said Kinoti

He observed many officers lacked proper intellectual training and were receiving backlash from the public because of poor training and negative perception over their academic background.

"Many people fail to appreciate the work of our officers do because of the perception that they are not well educated, this program will fill that gap," he added
