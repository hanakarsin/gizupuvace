Nyeri High School KCSE results, fees, location, contacts

Publish date: 2024-03-24

Nyeri High School is located within Nyeri County, a few kilometres from the county capital along Mathari road. The institution takes pride in being among the few in the country, offering an environment that is favourable for learning as well as self-development. With a history of excellent performance, Nyeri High School alumni comprise a range of prominent names in different sectors within the country.

The Catholic-sponsored facility first began as a primary boarding school for boys and has grown to become one of the best schools in the Mount Kenya region and the country at large. According to the Principal, Mr Maina J. K., Nyeri High is not just a place where learners pursue excellence in academics, but also a centre for motivating and empowering learners to think critically, learn continually, and become productive individuals in the current dynamic world. The motto "Elimu ni Ushindi" emphasizes on the importance of education in achieving all these.

Consolata Missionaries established the institution in 1924, at a time when only a handful of schools in the country were open for African learners. Later on, in 1947, the institution was upgraded to a Janitor Secondary School. The main contributor to this move was the rising number of Kenyans who longed for improved education standards in both primary and secondary schools.

It was until 1954 when the facility finally earned the status of a full secondary school. With the leadership of Rev. Fr. Lionel, the first lot of "O" level students sat for their national examination a year later. Throughout this period, the demand for improved learning facilities kept rising. As a result, the institution introduced the A-level so as to expand its reach. For decades, Nyeri High School performance has always been exemplary.

Most people ask, is Nyeri High School a national school? It is not. The facility is categorized as an Extra County School. Despite this, it has continuously earned recognition in different fields and co-curriculum activities, both nationally and internationally.

Nyeri High School KCSE results

Throughout its existence, the school has continually displayed exemplary performance. The same has been replicated in the 2019 results, where the leading candidate, Wahome Haron Mathenge, scored an A (plain) of 81 points.

For a more exhaustive highlight of Nyeri High School results for the year or previous performance, feel free to contact the institution.

Departments and subjects offered

Under the guidance of the school motto, learners get to embrace the broad curriculum, coping with vast topics in these five subject clusters:

  • Compulsory subjects – Mathematics, English, and Kiswahili
  • Sciences – Chemistry, Physics, and Biology
  • Humanities – Geography, History & Government, C.R.E
  • Group IV – Computer Studies and Agriculture
  • Group V – Business Studies, French, German

Sports and talents

The institution has facilities for a variety of sporting disciplines that learners can join. Allowing them to choose the game that they enjoy has earned the institution lots of success and accolades. The games master is in charge of coordinating every matter that relates to sporting activities and nurturing talent among the learners.

The Head of Clubs and Societies runs the schools 16 clubs with the assistance of patrons, and it is mandatory for learners to join at least one. Club meetings take place on Tuesdays, between 4 – 5 p.m., and are usually under the guidance of officials that have been voted in by fellow members.

The different clubs, sports, and associations usually organize regular trips where the boys get to interact with other learners. Also, students get to explore their talents on the annual Talent Day.

Student leadership and discipline

Nyeri High School prides itself as home to students who are not only morally-upright but religiously-conscious as well. Any discipline case within the facility is usually solved amicably, involving the right individuals to come up with the best solutions.

Each October, student get to select their preferred leaders. The learners are usually allowed to suggest the possible candidates for the numerous positions, then the staff vet the nominated names, and can reject an aspirant or add a new one. As a reward token, the student leaders may go on trips. Also, they can attend educative seminars as well as training to teach them the right balance between their obligations as leaders and educational pursuits. Every year, the school holds an official hand-over ceremony where prefects transfer leadership.

Prefects aside, students also enjoy apt guidance on different aspects by the school’s Guidance and Counseling Committee. Also comprising of the facility’s chaplaincy, the team is tasked with helping students make the right career choices and achieve success. Furthermore, they ensure learners are morally upright, growing to be a future generation that can be relied on.

Boarding facilities and health care

The dormitories are spacious enough for the over 1,000 students, with each governed by a Dorm Captain and Prefect. These two leaders are responsible for order within the students’ residences, as well as facilitating regular cleaning. Nutrition professionals, on the other hand, prepare the meals, keeping them balanced enough.

Admission criteria

Each academic year, Nyeri High School admits qualified learners from different corners of the country. One must display high academic performance and the yearning to complete their four years with exceptional performance. Notably, the learning environment here is exceedingly competitive; thus, scholars must learn to set their life goals and strive to reach them.

Currently, the Ministry of Education runs the entire selection process. But candidates can increase their chances of admission by listing the school among their top options for Extra County Schools.
