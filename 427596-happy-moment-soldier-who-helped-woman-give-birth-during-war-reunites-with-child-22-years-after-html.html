Happy Moment Soldier Who Helped Woman Give Birth During War Reunites With the Child 22 Years Later

Publish date: 2024-06-01
  • A soldier, Benjamin Farinazzo, has narrated how he reunited with the baby he helped birth more than two decades ago during a war
  • The family of the child named the baby after the man as a way to appreciate him for how he helped them
  • Twenty-two years after, the then baby contacted him, and Benjamin was able to see the young man face to face

A soldier, Benjamin Farinazzo, who helped a woman deliver a baby during war many years ago, has narrated the story of how it all happened.

He said that while he was still serving at war, a man and two women, one of whom was heavily pregnant came calling under heavy rain.

The soldier showed kindness

The soldier said that with the lifesaving skill he had been taught as a soldier, he could help the woman to a safe place.

However, it should be noted that they had been hiding for a month, where they only had to survive on roots as food.

After they took the woman to a makeshift medical centre, the man revealed that the woman had given birth on a hard and cold concrete floor.

What a happy reunion

As an appreciation, the family named the baby after him and called him Benjamin. But, unfortunately, after he left for his base, the soldier never saw the baby again.

Twenty-two years later, the baby contacted the military man via social media and told him he was the baby whose birth he had assisted more than two decades ago. It was a happy Facebook reunion.
