ANC claims Khalwale at pains to explain Matungu, Kabuchai by-election losses after spending &quot;billion

Publish date: 2024-07-25

- ANC chair Kelvin Lunani claimed Tanga Tanga politicians pocketed loads of money and were now lamenting after they lost in the by-elections

- ANC candidate, Peter Oscar Nabulindo won the Matungu by-election race after garnering 14,260 votes beating ODM's David Were and UDA's Alex Lanya

- The Kabuchai MP seat was taken by Ford Kenya's Majimbo Kalasinga who floored UDA's Evans Kakai

Amani National Congress (ANC) has ridiculed former Kakamega senator Boni Khalwale and Tanga Tanga politicians over failure to deliver the Matungu and Kabuchai Member of Parliament seats.

The Opposition outfit claimed, Khalwale, popularly referred to as the bullfighter, squandered 'billions' of shillings together with other Deputy President William Ruto's allies while pretending they were campaigning for United Democratic Alliance (UDA) candidates.

"Boni Khalwale and his corps are at pains to explain to their sponsor how they squandered billions under the guise of campaigning," a statement by ANC chairperson Kelvin Lunani read.

Tanga Tanga politicians allegedly minted "billions"

Lunani claimed Tanga Tanga politicians pocketed loads of money and were now lamenting after they lost in the by-elections.

"They pretended to campaign while spiriting cash into their pockets. ANC will not accept to be used in diversionary tactics. Let the UDA proponents account to their benefactor and stop seeking hideouts by scapegoating ANC," he added.

The Matungu by-election race was won by ANC candidate, Peter Oscar Nabulindo, who garnered 14,260 votes in the highly contested election.

David Were of the ODM party, a former lawmaker, came second with 10,565 votes while United Democratic Alliance (UDA) aspirant Alex Lanya was a distant third with 5,513 votes.

Voter bribery claims

The mini-poll was, however, marred by voter bribery claims with counter-accusations by participants.

The Kabuchai MP seat, on the other hand, was taken by Ford Kenya's Majimbo Kalasinga who secured 19,274 votes representing 65% of the total votes cast while his closest rival Evans Kakai of the United Democratic Alliance (UDA) was second with 6,455 votes.

Ford Kenya party leader Moses Wetang'ula had raised concerns about voter bribery in the constituency.

The Bungoma senator alleged UDA allied politicians were dishing out cash to voters in an effort to sway them.

"We have been informed by the various command posts in the constituency of voter bribery cases by UDA leaning MPs and politicians. The lawmakers have been spotted roaming around with sachets of money, others are from this county while others from elsewhere," Wetang'ula lamented.
