The PRSK awards for excellence are here again

Publish date: 2024-08-09

The Public Relations Society of Kenya has launched the 2016 awards for excellence.

The chief guest will be Joseph Mucheru, Kenya's cabinet secretary in the Ministry of Information, Communications and Technology.

Kenya’s finest professionals in the public relations field will once again be awarded at the 16th dinner edition of the 2016 PRSK awards set for Friday November 25, at the Intercontinental hotel in Nairobi.

“We intend to make award winning entries be published and available as case studies for use in learning institutions so be available to make history,” said Jane Gitau, PRSK chairperson.

Based on the theme ‘raising the bar – towards a gold standard in the public relations industry in Kenya’, the event has continuously endeavored to recognize and reward public relations practitioners who have showcased successful delivery of strategic, creative and professional public relations campaigns.

The awards are open to members and non-members drawn from business enterprises, associations, private and government bodies as well as students pursuing public relations and/or related studies. The PRSK Awards reflect the level of competitive services offered in the Kenya public relations industry and are aligned with the International Public Relations Association (IPRA) Golden Word Awards and SABRE Awards

The 2016 awards categories include:

PR CAMPAIGN CATEGORIES

1. Media Relations Campaign of the Year

2. Consumer Relations Campaign of the Year

3. Public Affairs Campaign of the Year

4. PR Event of the Year

5. Social Responsibility Campaign of the Year

6. Internal Communication Campaign of the Year

7. New Media PR Campaign of the Year

8. Public Sector Campaign of the Year

9. Not for Profit Campaign of the Year

SUPPLEMENTARY CATEGORIES

1. Corporate Publication of the Year

2. Young Communicator of the Year

HONORARY CATEGORIES

1. PRSK Golden Honors

2. PRSK Sheppard Honors

The awards are judged by a panel of industry experts drawn from PR and Communication, Education, Media and Marketing who question thoroughly the results against set objectives.

The panel also evaluates whether the campaign had a positive impact on the tripe bottom-line of the organization based on a four tier parameter that touches on research, planning, execution and evaluation.

We at Tuko congratulate the nominees and eventual winners of the awards.
