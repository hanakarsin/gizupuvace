5 Years On: Kibaki's Grandson Showcases Love Ex-President Had for Wife Lucy in Timeless Photo

Publish date: 2024-03-02

- Lucy Kibaki once slapped a master of ceremony during a Jamhuri Day dinner in 2007 hosted at State House for wrongly introducing her

- Lucy died in 2016, three years after her husband had served his second term as the president of Kenya

- Ever since his wife's death, retired Mwai Kibaki has been in and out of the hospital for undisclosed illness

- Kibaki's last public appearance was during the late former president Daniel Arap Moi’s funeral in February 2020

April 26 marked five years since former first lady Lucy Kibaki passed on.

Lucy, the wife of retired president Mwai Kibaki, died on April 26, 2016, while receiving treatment at Bupa Cromwell Hospital in London, UK.

Lucy was 80 at the time she passed away. The former first lady came to the spotlight for slapping a cameraman in 2005, who was filming her when she stormed Nation Media Group offices on Kimathi Street after publishing a story about her.

The former first lady was known for her public dramas, and remembering the day she passed away, her grandson Mwai Kibaki Jnr shared a photo she took with her hubby (Mwai Kibaki) many moons ago.

"She is water.Powerful enough to drown you.Soft enough to cleanse you.Deep enough to save you.26.04.16 ❤️" he captioned the photo.

Lucy and Kibaki had four children; three sons and a daughter namely Jimmy Kibaki, David Kagai, Tony Githinji and Judy Wanjiku.

Kibaki served as the third president of Kenya from December 2002 to April 2013

Five months ago, TUKO.co.ke published an article on how Kenyans trooped to social media to celebrate Kibaki on his birthday as he turned 89.

Kenyans heaped praises on the former head of state for reviving the country’s economy when he took over from Moi.

In 2003, Kibaki introduced free primary education for all Kenyan children.
