President Uhuru Kenyatta on Tuesday, August 10, held a closed-door meeting with former NASA coalition principles.

Uhuru met with NASA leaders

According to the State House, the leaders had a consultative meeting to discuss several subjects touching on the nation's state.

Political party leaders present were Raila Odinga (ODM), Musalia Mudavadi (ANC), Kalonzo Musyoka (Wiper), Moses Wetangula (Ford Kenya), and Gideon Moi (KANU).

"His Excellency President Uhuru Kenyatta on Tuesday held a consultative meeting with a cross-section of political party leaders to discuss several subjects touching on the State of the Kenyan Nation," the statement read.

The meeting held at State House, Mombasa, was also attended by Kakamega governor and ODM deputy party leader Wycliffe Oparanya.

"Among the subjects discussed by the leaders were COVID-19's impact on the country and how to revamp the economy; and the importance of maintaining peace, unity and national cohesion," they added.

Uhuru calls NASA to unite

On several occasions, Uhuru has been calling for the former NASA co-principal to be united ahead of the much anticipated 2022 General Election.

Uhuru hinted at supporting one of the principals ahead of 2022 polls.

On March 16, the president met the political big wigs led by Raila, Mudavadi, Charity Ngilu, Moses Wetang'ula and Kalonzo Musyoka at the State House Nairobi, where he urged them to unite.

During the meeting, Uhuru asked the leaders to bury the hatchet for the sake of the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) and the coming referendum.

A few months ago, Gideon Moi also pleaded with principals to unite and work together ahead of the 2022 General Election.

"Musalia Mudavadi, Kalonzo itsogether," he said.

Betrayal in NASA Coalition

Mudavadi, Kalonzo and Wetang'ula were the first to pull out the coalition, which was meant to last beyond 2022.

The three accused Raila of reneging on his promise of endorsing one of them for the presidency in 2022, in line with the NASA coalition agreement of 2017.

They also blamed the former prime minister for perpetuating politics of mistrust, conmanship and deceit.

On the other hand, the ODM leader, who later disowned the NASA coalition pact, accused the three partners of betrayal, cowardice and 'parasitic habits'.
